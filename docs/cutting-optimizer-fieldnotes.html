02

The Cut Itself

This is the part that actually changes the math. Miss it and every cutting list comes out a few millimetres short, across a whole batch.

Why it's tricky: mitred pieces and square-cut pieces are measured differently — a mitred corner needs extra length that a butt joint doesn't. If they mix both in one product, ask which parts use which.

07

Glossary — Quick Reference

The six words most likely to come up. Skim once before you walk in.

KERF
Material lost to the width of the saw blade on every single cut.
NESTING
Arranging multiple required pieces onto one stock bar to minimise the leftover.
OFFCUT / REMNANT
The leftover piece after a cut — reusable later if it's long enough.
MITRE CUT
An angled cut, usually 45°, used to join two pieces at a corner.
YIELD / UTILIZATION %
The share of stock material actually used, versus wasted as offcut.
BATCH
A group of jobs cut together so they can share stock bars efficiently.
